City Resilience Program

CRP catalyzes a shift from a primarily siloed, single-stream city-level resilience operations approach to longer term, more comprehensive, multi-disciplinary packages of technical and financial services, building the pipeline for viable projects at the city level that build resilient cities with the capacity to plan for and mitigate adverse impacts of disasters and climate change, thus enabling them to save lives, reduce losses, and unlock economic and social potential.

 Steven Rubinyi
 srubinyi@worldbank.org
 CRP Webpage

Capacity

    Team
  • ~15 people
    Workshops/Events
  • Capacity building through urban resilience enhancing workshops every 6 months for staff, country offices, and clients

Services Offered

  • Rapid assessments of the critical development challenges that cities face using the best publicly available global geospatial datasets and open-source tools. Packages of geospatial solutions, maps and data visualizations that integrate the features of both the built and natural environments. The ability to overlay or simultaneously display multiple layers of spatial information in a dynamic mapping environment and identify areas of overlap. Facilitation of workshops that drive consensus on climate, disaster, and resilience issues across stakeholders and advance upstream scoping of urban resilience investments.
  • CRP provides support in designing IPF components around spatial data infrastructure. It helps in creating new views on the nature and location of potential investments, and drive alignment with clients in investment discussions.

Main Products

  • City Scans
  • Resilient Capital Investment Planning and Financing Workshops

Key Partnerships

    Internal
  • GFDRR, GOST, ITS, several KSBs, and other World Bank teams
